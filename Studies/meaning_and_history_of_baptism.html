The Meaning and History of Baptism

A Study of God's Way and Man's Changes

Baptism is a very important part of the Christian life. It is like a door that people walk through to join God's family. The Bible teaches us that baptism is a holy act that shows we have left our old life behind and started a new life with Jesus Christ. This study looks at how baptism began, what it really means, and how some parts of it were changed by human traditions over time.

The Foundation: Jesus Christ as Our Example

The story of Christian baptism starts with Jesus. Before He began His work on earth, He went to the Jordan River to be baptized by His cousin, John the Baptist. John was a man sent by God to tell people to turn away from their sins and get ready for the Savior.

When Jesus came to the river, John did not want to baptize Him at first. He knew Jesus had never sinned. But Jesus told him it was the right thing to do to "fulfill all righteousness". By doing this, Jesus showed us exactly what we should do. He went down into the water, and when He came up, the Holy Spirit came down like a dove, and God the Father spoke from heaven, saying He was well pleased with His Son. Jesus did not need to be washed from sin, but He was baptized to show us the way.

The True Mode: Baptism by Immersion

The word "baptism" comes from an old Greek word, baptizo. This word means to "immerse," "submerge," or to "make fully wet". In the Bible, baptism was always done by putting the whole person under the water.

When the apostles started the early church, they followed this same pattern. For example, when Philip baptized a man from Ethiopia, they both went down into the water, and then they both came up out of the water. Early church records and writings show that for many hundreds of years, the whole world practiced baptism by immersion. It was only much later that people started using other ways that were not in the Bible.

The Spiritual Meaning: Death and Resurrection

Baptism is more than just getting wet. It is a powerful symbol of what happens in a person’s heart. The Bible says that when we are baptized, we are "buried with Christ".

Going under the water is like being put in a "watery grave." It shows that our old life of sin is dead and buried. Coming up out of the water shows that we are being "raised" to live a new life, just as Jesus was raised from the dead. It is a public promise to leave behind pride and selfishness and to live for God. When someone is baptized, the three great powers of heaven—the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit—are there as witnesses to this special promise.

How the Truth Was Corrupted: From Immersion to Sprinkling

As time went on, the devil tried to hide the true meaning of baptism. Just as he changed the Sabbath, he also worked to change baptism from immersion to "sprinkling" or "pouring".

During the middle ages, the church started to follow human traditions instead of the Bible. They began to believe that the act of baptism itself saved a person, even if the person didn't understand it. Because they were afraid that babies might die without being saved, they started "infant baptism" and used sprinkling because it was easier than putting a baby under water. James White and other early pioneers pointed out that sprinkling for baptism is a "counterfeit" and an "error" that came from the Catholic church and was kept by many other churches.

Who Should Be Baptized? Faith and the Vow

The Bible teaches that baptism is for people who have heard the gospel, believe in Jesus, and have repented of their sins. This is called "Believer's Baptism."

A baby cannot choose to follow Jesus or understand what sin is. Therefore, sprinkling babies is not found in the Bible. In the early church, people made a "vow" or a promise at their baptism to disown the devil and follow Christ. This promise requires the person to be old enough to make a real choice. Baptism is a sign that a person has entered into a personal relationship with Christ.

The Baptism of the Holy Spirit

There are two parts to being born again: being born of water and being born of the Spirit. Water baptism is the outward sign, but the baptism of the Holy Spirit is the inward power.

The Bible speaks of "early rain" and "latter rain." The early rain was the outpouring of the Holy Spirit at Pentecost, which gave the first Christians power to preach. The latter rain is a special gift of the Spirit that will be given to God's people right before Jesus comes back. Every Christian needs to seek the daily baptism of the Holy Spirit to grow and stay strong against temptation.

Re-baptism and the Restoration of Truth

Sometimes people learn new truths from the Bible that they did not know when they were first baptized. For example, if someone was sprinkled as a baby, they may choose to be baptized the Bible way (by immersion) when they grow up and choose to follow Jesus for themselves.

Early Seventh-day Adventist pioneers often practiced re-baptism when they discovered new light, like the truth about the seventh-day Sabbath. They believed it was a way to show a deeper commitment to following all of God's commandments. Today, re-baptism is also seen as a good step for people who have walked away from God and want to come back to Him with a fresh start.

Timeline of Baptism in History

  • The Ministry of John (A.D. 27): John the Baptist begins baptizing people in the river to prepare them for Jesus.
  • Jesus’ Baptism: Jesus is baptized by immersion as our example.
  • The Great Commission: Jesus commands His followers to go into all the world and baptize people in the name of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.
  • The Apostolic Church: Thousands are baptized by immersion after hearing the gospel (Acts 2:41).
  • The Entrance of Error: Over many centuries, the church begins to use sprinkling and baptize infants instead of believers.
  • The Reformation: Reformers start to bring the Bible back to the people, though many still keep the practice of infant baptism.
  • Restoring the Truth: Groups like the Baptists and later the Seventh-day Adventists return to the Bible way of baptizing only believers by full immersion.

Conclusion

The history of baptism shows us that God's ways are simple and full of meaning. While the devil has tried to hide the truth behind human ceremonies and traditions, the Bible remains the clear guide. By following Jesus into the water of baptism, we show the world that we belong to Him and are ready for His soon return.
