Where Is Jesus Right Now — And What Is He Doing for Our World Today?

A Biblical and Spirit of Prophecy Study on Christ's Present Ministry

1. Our Value According to Scripture

The Bible does not define human worth by success, intelligence, influence, wealth, morality, or productivity. It defines human value by what God was willing to give to redeem humanity.

John 3:16 (KJV) For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life.
Romans 5:8 (KJV) But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us.
Romans 8:32 (KJV) He that spared not his own Son, but delivered him up for us all, how shall he not with him also freely give us all things?

These verses establish a single truth:

Human worth is measured by the sacrifice of Christ.

Ellen G. White writes:

"In the gift of His Son for our redemption, God has shown how high a value He places upon the human soul." — Steps to Christ, p. 21

This is not poetic language. It is theological fact.

Heaven assessed humanity's condition, and the price required for restoration was the life of God's Son.

2. The Ascension of Christ and His Present Location

After His resurrection, Jesus ascended bodily into heaven.

Acts 1:9–11 (KJV) And when he had spoken these things, while they beheld, he was taken up; and a cloud received him out of their sight. And while they looked stedfastly toward heaven as he went up, behold, two men stood by them in white apparel; Which also said, Ye men of Galilee, why stand ye gazing up into heaven? this same Jesus, which is taken up from you into heaven, shall so come in like manner as ye have seen him go into heaven.

Scripture clearly states where Christ is now:

Hebrews 8:1–2 (KJV) Now of the things which we have spoken this is the sum: We have such an high priest, who is set on the right hand of the throne of the Majesty in the heavens; A minister of the sanctuary, and of the true tabernacle, which the Lord pitched, and not man.

Christ is currently:

  • In heaven
  • Seated at the right hand of God
  • Serving as High Priest
  • Ministering in the heavenly sanctuary

Ellen White confirms:

"The sanctuary in heaven is the very center of Christ's work in behalf of men." — The Great Controversy, p. 488

3. The Nature of Christ's Present Work

Scripture identifies Christ's current role as intercessory ministry.

Hebrews 7:25 (KJV) Wherefore he is able also to save them to the uttermost that come unto God by him, seeing he ever liveth to make intercession for them.
1 John 2:1–2 (KJV) My little children, these things write I unto you, that ye sin not. And if any man sin, we have an advocate with the Father, Jesus Christ the righteous: And he is the propitiation for our sins: and not for ours only, but also for the sins of the whole world.

Christ functions as:

  • Advocate
  • Intercessor
  • Mediator
  • High Priest
  • Representative of humanity before God

Ellen White writes:

"Jesus stands in the presence of God, offering His blood for sinners." — Early Writings, p. 38

This ministry is continuous, not symbolic.

4. Christ's Work in Relation to the Final Judgment

Scripture teaches that a pre-Advent judgment takes place in heaven.

Daniel 7:9–10 (KJV) I beheld till the thrones were cast down, and the Ancient of days did sit, whose garment was white as snow, and the hair of his head like the pure wool: his throne was like the fiery flame, and his wheels as burning fire. A fiery stream issued and came forth from before him: thousand thousands ministered unto him, and ten thousand times ten thousand stood before him: the judgment was set, and the books were opened.
Revelation 14:6–7 (KJV) And I saw another angel fly in the midst of heaven, having the everlasting gospel to preach unto them that dwell on the earth... Saying with a loud voice, Fear God, and give glory to him; for the hour of his judgment is come...

Christ Himself is both Judge and Advocate.

John 5:22 (KJV) For the Father judgeth no man, but hath committed all judgment unto the Son.

Ellen White writes:

"As books of record are opened in the judgment, the lives of all who have believed on Jesus come in review before God." — The Great Controversy, p. 482

Yet Christ's presence in judgment is not for condemnation, but for salvation.

Hebrews 9:24 (KJV) For Christ is not entered into the holy places made with hands, which are the figures of the true; but into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God for us.

5. Christ's Daily Work for the World

Scripture reveals that Christ actively sustains all creation.

Colossians 1:16–17 (KJV) For by him were all things created... and he is before all things, and by him all things consist.

Christ restrains chaos, limits destruction, and preserves human probation.

2 Thessalonians 2:6–7 (KJV) And now ye know what withholdeth that he might be revealed in his time. For the mystery of iniquity doth already work: only he who now letteth will let, until he be taken out of the way.

Ellen White writes:

"But for the restraining power of God, the world would be filled with violence and crime." — Education, p. 179

6. Christ's Personal Ministry to Every Believer

Scripture emphasizes Christ's intimate involvement in the believer's life.

Hebrews 4:14–16 (KJV) Seeing then that we have a great high priest, that is passed into the heavens, Jesus the Son of God, let us hold fast our profession. For we have not an high priest which cannot be touched with the feeling of our infirmities; but was in all points tempted like as we are, yet without sin. Let us therefore come boldly unto the throne of grace, that we may obtain mercy, and find grace to help in time of need.
Psalm 34:15 (KJV) The eyes of the LORD are upon the righteous, and his ears are open unto their cry.
1 Peter 5:7 (KJV) Casting all your care upon him; for he careth for you.

Ellen White writes:

"Christ is acquainted with all our trials, all our sorrows, and all our temptations." — Steps to Christ, p. 100

7. Christ's Use of Angels in Human Affairs

Hebrews 1:14 (KJV) Are they not all ministering spirits, sent forth to minister for them who shall be heirs of salvation?
Psalm 34:7 (KJV) The angel of the LORD encampeth round about them that fear him, and delivereth them.

Ellen White writes:

"Angels are sent on missions of mercy, to minister to those who shall be heirs of salvation." — The Desire of Ages, p. 143

8. Christ's Work for His Church

Ephesians 5:25–27 (KJV) Husbands, love your wives, even as Christ also loved the church, and gave himself for it; That he might sanctify and cleanse it with the washing of water by the word, That he might present it to himself a glorious church, not having spot, or wrinkle, or any such thing; but that it should be holy and without blemish.

Ellen White writes:

"God has a church upon the earth who are His chosen people." — Testimonies for the Church, Vol. 3, p. 15

9. Our Cooperation with Christ

Philippians 2:12–13 (KJV) Wherefore, my beloved, as ye have always obeyed... work out your own salvation with fear and trembling. For it is God which worketh in you both to will and to do of his good pleasure.
Revelation 3:20 (KJV) Behold, I stand at the door, and knock: if any man hear my voice, and open the door, I will come in to him, and will sup with him, and he with me.

Ellen White writes:

"Consecrate yourself to God in the morning; make this your very first work." — Steps to Christ, p. 70

10. God: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit

Deuteronomy 6:4 (KJV) Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God is one LORD.
Matthew 28:19 (KJV) Go ye therefore, and teach all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Ghost.
John 10:30 (KJV) I and my Father are one.

Ellen White writes:

"The three great powers of heaven are pledged to cooperate with those who seek to live the new life in Christ." — Special Testimonies, Series B, No. 7

Final Summary

Jesus Christ is:

  • Alive
  • Active
  • Interceding
  • Judging
  • Sustaining
  • Saving

Christianity is not a memory of past events.
It is participation in a present heavenly ministry.

Hebrews 12:24 (KJV) And to Jesus the mediator of the new covenant, and to the blood of sprinkling, that speaketh better things than that of Abel.
