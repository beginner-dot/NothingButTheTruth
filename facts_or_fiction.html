Manuscript Evidence

The Bible is supported by thousands of ancient manuscripts, making it the best-attested book of antiquity. The consistency among these manuscripts demonstrates the careful preservation of the biblical text.

Key Manuscript Evidence

  • The Dead Sea Scrolls: Discovered between 1947 and 1956 near Qumran, these ancient manuscripts include portions of every Old Testament book except Esther. They date from the third century BC to the first century AD and confirm the accuracy of the Hebrew Scriptures. Reference: Dead Sea Scrolls ‚Äì Wikipedia
  • The Codex Sinaiticus: One of the oldest and most complete manuscripts of the Greek Bible, dating to the 4th century AD. It contains the entire New Testament and much of the Old Testament. Reference: Codex Sinaiticus ‚Äì Wikipedia
  • The Codex Vaticanus: Another important 4th-century Greek manuscript containing most of the Old and New Testaments. It is considered one of the best witnesses to the original biblical text. Reference: Codex Vaticanus ‚Äì Wikipedia
  • The Chester Beatty Papyri: A collection of early papyrus manuscripts of the New Testament, dating from the 2nd to 4th centuries AD, providing valuable evidence for the early transmission of the biblical text. Reference: Chester Beatty Papyri ‚Äì Wikipedia
  • The John Rylands Papyrus (P52): The earliest known fragment of the New Testament, containing a portion of the Gospel of John and dated to around 125 AD. Reference: Rylands Library Papyrus P52 ‚Äì Wikipedia
