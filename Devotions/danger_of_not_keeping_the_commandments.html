⚖️ The Danger of Not Keeping the Commandments

A solemn call to repentance, obedience, and the faith of Jesus

A Loving Warning, Not a Harsh Threat

God gave His commandments because He loves us. They are not chains. They are protection. They are not against freedom. They are the path to true freedom.

In the Seventh-day Adventist message, we preach both grace and obedience. We are not saved by law-keeping. We are saved by Jesus Christ alone. But the saved life will show loyalty to God’s law through the power of the Holy Spirit.

“Fear God, and keep His commandments: for this is the whole duty of man.” — Ecclesiastes 12:13

What Is the Real Danger?

1) A Numb Conscience

When people repeatedly break God’s commandments, the heart becomes less sensitive to sin. What once felt wrong starts feeling normal. That is spiritual danger.

2) A False Gospel

Some say, “I love Jesus, but commandments do not matter.” Scripture rejects that idea.

“If ye love me, keep my commandments.” — John 14:15

True grace does not cancel obedience. True grace teaches obedience.

3) End-Time Deception

Adventist preaching is clear: the final crisis is about worship, loyalty, and authority. Revelation describes God’s last-day people this way:

“Here are they that keep the commandments of God, and the faith of Jesus.” — Revelation 14:12

The issue is not only belief with the lips. The issue is allegiance in life.

The Seventh-day Adventist Prophetic Burden

We preach the three angels’ messages because time is short and souls are precious. The Sabbath, the seventh day, remains God’s holy sign of His Creatorship and authority. In a world of confusion, God still calls people to reverence, obedience, and truth.

The commandments do not compete with the cross. They lead us to the cross, where we receive forgiveness, and from the cross we receive power to walk in newness of life.

Ellen G. White wrote in public-domain works that God’s law is a transcript of His character and that the final conflict centers on worship and obedience. Her counsel consistently points believers to Christ, repentance, and faithful commandment-keeping. See: The Great Controversy, Patriarchs and Prophets (public domain)

Practical Signs You Are Drifting

  • You no longer feel conviction when you break clear biblical truth.
  • You treat God’s name and holy things carelessly.
  • You replace Sabbath rest with convenience and business.
  • You defend disobedience instead of confessing it.
  • You speak of grace but resist surrender.

Hope for the Backslider

If you have drifted, there is still mercy. Jesus is our High Priest in the heavenly sanctuary. He intercedes for sinners who repent. No one is beyond restoration.

“If we confess our sins, He is faithful and just to forgive us our sins, and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness.” — 1 John 1:9

A Direct Appeal

Do not harden your heart. Do not delay obedience. The world is moving quickly toward final events. Stand with Jesus now. Keep His commandments through His grace. Let your life preach the everlasting gospel with power.

Prayer

“Lord Jesus, forgive my compromise. Write Your law in my heart. Give me courage to obey You in every area of life. Keep me faithful in these last days, and fill me with the faith of Jesus. Amen.”
