🤝 How the Ten Commandments Connect Us with God

Not just rules — a relationship path

More Than Rules

Many people see commandments as cold rules. But in the Bible, God gave the commandments after delivering His people from Egypt. First rescue, then instruction. First grace, then direction.

This means the commandments are relational. They teach us how to stay close to God and how to treat people made in His image.

“And hereby we do know that we know him, if we keep his commandments.” — 1 John 2:3

Commandment and Connection

In everyday language, a commandment is an authoritative instruction that guides behavior. In spiritual life, it becomes a bridge: what God says shows us who God is.

The First Four: Vertical Connection

  • No other gods: God asks for your full trust, not divided loyalty.
  • No idols: God wants your heart, not substitutes.
  • Honor His name: God invites reverence, not careless speech.
  • Remember Sabbath: God gives sacred time for worship, rest, and renewal.

The Last Six: Horizontal Connection

  • Honor parents
  • Protect life
  • Be faithful
  • Do not steal
  • Do not lie
  • Do not covet

When these are lived sincerely, peace grows. Homes become safer. Churches become healthier. Hearts become softer toward God.

Ellen G. White explains in public-domain writings that God’s law is a transcript of His character and that true obedience is the fruit of love, not forced religion (see Steps to Christ and Patriarchs and Prophets). Public-domain EGW writings

Simple Daily Practice

  • Morning: Read one commandment and ask, “What does this show me about God’s heart?”
  • Midday: In one difficult moment, choose obedience over impulse.
  • Evening: Thank God for one victory and confess one failure honestly.

Prayer

“Father, connect my heart to Yours through Your commandments. Teach me to obey from love, not from pressure. Help me walk with Jesus in truth, humility, and joy. Amen.”
