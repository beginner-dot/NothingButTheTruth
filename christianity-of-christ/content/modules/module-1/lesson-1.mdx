# Christ as the First Christian

## Bible Foundation

**Scripture**  
> Luke 2:40-52; John 5:19; 1 Peter 2:21

Christ is called first here not as a convert but as the original embodiment of true Godward human life. He trusted the Father, loved people sacrificially, obeyed from the heart, and walked in the Spirit’s power. Christian life is therefore not invented by culture; it is received from Christ’s life and ministry.

EGW repeatedly presents Christ as both substitute and example: He saves us fully, then forms us progressively into His likeness.

## Historical Context

Early Christian proclamation centered on Christ’s person and pattern. Believers were called to follow Him in dependence, humility, service, and obedience rooted in love. Public-domain records reflect this Christ-formed identity as foundational to Christian communities.

## Practical Application

To say “Christ is the First Christian” means modern disciples evaluate priorities, speech, work, relationships, and mission through the lens of Christ’s life. Every habit asks: does this reflect Jesus?

## Accountability

Establish one weekly discipleship review around four questions: prayer, Scripture, love in action, and witness.

## Reflection Prompts
- What in Christ’s life most confronts my current habits?
- Where am I tempted to imitate culture more than Christ?
- Which relationship needs Christlike humility?
- How can I respond to pressure without losing love?
- What one obedience step is the Spirit inviting now?

## Practical Exercises
- Read one gospel chapter daily and identify one action of Christ to imitate.
- Pray John 15 each day this week.
- Serve one vulnerable person intentionally.
- Practice one conversation marked by truth and gentleness.
- Write a personal “imitate Christ” commitment statement.

## Daily Habit Suggestions
- Morning surrender prayer
- Gospel-focused Scripture reading
- One act of practical compassion
- Evening accountability review

## Quiz (7 Questions)
1. **MCQ:** Why is Christ called the First Christian in this course? A) Chronological church member B) Full model of Spirit-led human faithfulness C) Historical slogan D) Cultural title.  
   **Answer:** B.
2. **MCQ:** John 5:19 emphasizes: A) Independence from the Father B) Union and dependence C) Isolation D) Ritualism.  
   **Answer:** B.
3. **MCQ:** 1 Peter 2:21 teaches believers to: A) Avoid growth B) Follow Christ’s steps C) Trust self D) Reject service.  
   **Answer:** B.
4. **Scenario:** You feel spiritually dry. Best next step? **Answer:** Return to abiding practices—prayer, Word, and honest accountability.
5. **Scenario:** You are wronged publicly. Christlike response? **Answer:** Truth with meekness, forgiveness, and faithful witness.
6. **True/False:** Christ’s example replaces grace. **Answer:** False.
7. **Short Answer:** Name one concrete way you will imitate Christ in the next 48 hours.

## Theological Guardrail

Justification is by grace through faith in Christ alone. Sanctification is Spirit-enabled conformity to Christ. We do not follow Christ to be accepted; we follow Christ because we are accepted in Him.
