# Introduction to the Christianity of Christ

## Bible Foundation

**Scripture**  
> Matthew 4:1-11; John 13:34-35; John 15:1-5

The Christianity of Christ means more than religious identity. It means receiving life from Christ and learning to live as He lived: surrendered to the Father, filled by the Spirit, rooted in Scripture, and active in love.

## Spirit of Prophecy

> [Insert EGW public-domain excerpt with citation and link placeholder]

The Spirit of Prophecy consistently directs attention to Christ as both the basis of acceptance before God and the pattern of transformed living.

## Historical Context

Early Christian communities saw Jesus not only as Savior but as the living pattern of discipleship. Their worship, fellowship, and mission were framed by imitation of Christ in dependence on the Holy Spirit. Public-domain records consistently show Christ-centered formation as the core of Christian identity.

## Practical Application

This course invites daily practices that support spiritual formation: prayer, Scripture, service, and accountability. These are not burdens to earn favor; they are pathways for abiding in Christ.

## Accountability

Choose one trusted believer and schedule a weekly check-in for honesty, encouragement, and prayer.

## Reflection Prompts
- What does Christ-centered Christianity correct in my current view of faith?
- Where do I rely on effort instead of grace?
- Which habit most helps me abide in Christ?
- What relationship needs Christlike love this week?
- How can I respond to failure without shame but with repentance?

## Practical Exercises
- Pray 10 minutes each morning for 7 days.
- Read one gospel chapter daily and summarize Christ’s character.
- Perform one hidden act of service.
- Journal one area of surrender each evening.
- Ask one mentor/friend for prayer accountability.

## Daily Habit Suggestions
- Prayer
- Scripture meditation
- Service
- Solitude and reflection

## Quiz (7 Questions)
1. **MCQ:** The Christianity of Christ is primarily: A) Rule keeping B) Union with Christ by grace C) Social identity D) Spiritual performance.  
   **Answer:** B.
2. **MCQ:** Which habit best supports abiding in Christ? A) Comparison B) Prayerful Scripture meditation C) Isolation D) Anxiety.  
   **Answer:** B.
3. **MCQ:** Christian love in John 13 is: A) Optional B) Cultural C) Defining evidence of discipleship D) Impossible.  
   **Answer:** C.
4. **Scenario:** You fail a commitment. Best response? **Answer:** Repent, receive grace, continue in accountability.
5. **Scenario:** A friend is struggling spiritually. Best response? **Answer:** Listen, pray, share Scripture, and walk with them.
6. **True/False:** Obedience is fruit of grace, not the price of salvation. **Answer:** True.
7. **Short Answer:** What one step will help you abide in Christ this week?

## Theological Guardrail

**Salvation by grace through faith** is affirmed (Ephesians 2:8-10). **Justification** is Christ’s gift. **Sanctification** is Spirit-led transformation over time.
